Fibrinogen:
Fibrinogen is a blood plasma protein that is produced in the liver and is responsible for normal blood clotting.
Fibrinogen is an acute phase reactant.
High levels of fibrinogen are associated with an increased risk for cardiovascular disease.
Increased levels are also found during pregnancy, oral contraceptive use, inflammatory disorders (like rheumatoid arthritis
and glomerulonephritis), trauma, acute infections and certain cancers.
Acutely low levels are seen in:
• DIC
• Abnormal Fibrinolysis
• Rapid, large volume blood transfusions
Chronically low levels are seen in:
• Inherited conditions - afibrinogenemia hypofibrinogenemia
