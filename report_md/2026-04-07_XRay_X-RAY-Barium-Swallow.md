## Summary (extracted)

Clinical details: Post vocal cord lateralisation.
Observation:
There was free flow of barium from the oral cavity to the stomach.
A thin rim of barium was seen into the right trachea-bronchial tree, suggestive of
aspiration.
The pyriform sinuses and the valleculae are normal.
A thin streak of barium was seen into the right tracheobronchial tree, suggestive of
aspiration.
There is no evidence of an oesophageal stricture.
No abnormal filling defects are seen in the oesophagus.
The gastro-esophageal junction is normal.
Impression:
A thin streak of barium is seen into the right tracheo-bronchial tree- suggestive of
aspiration.
Incidental note is made of patchy consolidates in the right upper zone and both midzones.
