## Summary (extracted)

CT Angiography - Pulmonary
Technique: CT Pulmonary Angiography was performed following bolus tracking after
intravenous administration of 40ml of contrast. Sagittal and coronal reformatted and MIP
images were obtained.
Clinical details: Patient developed breathlessness and cough since 2-3 days. Developed
hypoxia yesterday.
Findings:
Pulmonary arteries are dilated. The pulmonary trunk measures 35 mm. The right pulmonary
artery measures 25 mm and left pulmonary artery measures 25 mm in calibre.
A saddle thrombus is seen extending from the main pulmonary trunk into the right and left
main pulmonary artery branches.
On the left side, the filling defect is further seen extending into the upper and lower lobar
segmental and subsegmental branches. Completely occluding thrombus is noted in proximal
left upper lobar segmental branch;
Partially obstructive filling defects are seen in the right distal pulmonary artery, segmental
and sub-segmental arterial branches of superior and all the basal segments [anterior,
medial, posterior, lateral] of right lower lobe; Partially obstructive filling defects in all
segmental and sub-segmental arterial branches of right middle and sub-segmental branches
of right upper lobe.
Bilateral moderate pleural effusion is seen with partial subsegmental collapse of both lower
lobes and lingular segment.
The visualised lung parenchyma reveals mosaic attenuation.
The right atrium and ventricle is mildly dilated. The interventricular septum is straight.
Visualized abdomen shows, two non-enhancing hypodense lesions noted in the right lobe of
liver, largest ~3.2 x 2.8 cm with lobulated margins- benign in aetiology. Mild perisplenic fluid
is noted. Tip of nasogastric tube is seen in duodenum.
